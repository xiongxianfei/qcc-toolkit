## Presentation pattern

Use these source notes as the reviewable contract for the generated PowerPoint template.
The deck includes a data-entry slide with editable check items and tally columns.
The final slide should explain what was counted, where and when data was collected, and what notes affect later analysis.

## Method kit sections

### Purpose

Collect QCC observations consistently so baseline facts can be reviewed before analysis.

### QCC stage fit

Understand Current Condition. Use before Pareto, Histogram, trend review, or stratification.

### When to use

Use when the team needs repeatable counts by category, location, shift, time period, or another agreed stratification.

### When not to use

Do not use it when the observation definition is unclear, categories overlap, or collectors cannot apply the same rules.

### Required inputs

Observation definition, check items, tally columns, collection rules, collector or source, date range, filters, and review notes.

### Completed demo example

The deck includes a completed DEMO EXAMPLE - not project evidence that shows realistic tally data.

### Blank working slide or worksheet

The deck includes a blank working slide or worksheet users can copy into a QCC project deck.

### Interpretation patterns

Use patterns for most frequent item, visible pattern by time/location/shift, next analysis action, and caution wording that occurrence does not prove root cause.

### Common mistakes

Avoid changing categories midstream, missing source or date range, unclear observation definitions, mixed periods, and demo content used as project evidence.

### Facilitator checklist

Check that the observation definition is clear, source and date range are shown, categories are stable, totals are reviewed, the key conclusion is written, and the next action is clear.

### Python assist decision

PowerPoint is enough for teaching, draft, and small manual tally sheets.
Python is not normally needed unless large exported logs, repeated summaries, validation, or high-rigor evidence are required.

### Evidence/source note

Record source, date range, collection rules, filters, collector, review notes, prepared-by/date, and whether evidence level is teaching/draft, normal QCC project, competition or management review, or audit/high-risk.
