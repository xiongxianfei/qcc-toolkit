## Presentation pattern

Use these source notes as the reviewable contract for the generated PowerPoint template.
The deck includes a data-entry slide with editable cause statements and evidence notes.
The final slide should separate suspected causes from causes that have supporting evidence.

## Method kit sections

### Purpose

Organize suspected causes for one effect before verification.

### QCC stage fit

Analyze Causes. Use after the problem and baseline evidence are clear.

### When to use

Use when the team needs to brainstorm and organize many possible causes before verification.

### When not to use

Do not use it as proof that listed causes are real or when the effect statement is vague or mixed.

### Required inputs

Effect statement, cause branches, suspected causes, evidence notes, source, date range or session date, assumptions, and verification plan.

### Completed demo example

The deck includes a completed DEMO EXAMPLE - not project evidence that shows realistic suspected causes.

### Blank working slide or worksheet

The deck includes a blank working slide or worksheet users can copy into a QCC project deck.

### Interpretation patterns

Use patterns for cause map summary, verification decision, next action, and caution wording that listed causes are hypotheses until verified.

### Common mistakes

Avoid mixing several effects, listing symptoms as causes, marking causes verified without evidence, missing source or date range, and demo content used as project evidence.

### Facilitator checklist

Check that the effect statement matches evidence, source and date range or session date are shown, suspected and verified causes are separated, the key conclusion is written, and the next action is clear.

### Python assist decision

PowerPoint is enough for teaching, draft, and normal Fishbone diagram editing.
Python is not normally needed unless verification data needs summarization, validation, or high-rigor evidence packaging.

### Evidence/source note

Record source, date range or session date, assumptions, verification status, prepared-by/date, and whether evidence level is teaching/draft, normal QCC project, competition or management review, or audit/high-risk.
