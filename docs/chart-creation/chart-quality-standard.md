# Chart Quality Standard

## Purpose

This standard defines the minimum quality bar for QCC charts created from method guides.
It applies to manually created charts and to charts created with validated analysis paths.
Generated images may demonstrate chart concepts, but they are not final quantitative evidence.

## Required quality fields

Every final data-dependent QCC chart should satisfy these checks:

| Field | Standard |
|---|---|
| clear title | The title states the quality question, process, period, and chart purpose where practical. |
| correct chart type | The selected chart type matches the method and the decision being supported. |
| readable labels | Axis labels, category names, units, legends, and notes are readable in the intended review format. |
| source note | The chart identifies the source data, date range, scope or filters, and total sample or count when applicable. |
| correct scale | Axes and scales do not exaggerate, hide, or distort the pattern. |
| appropriate annotations | Annotations explain important changes, cutoffs, or review decisions without cluttering the chart. |
| defensible interpretation | The stated conclusion follows from the data, method logic, and evidence level. |

## Review questions

- Does the chart answer a specific QCC question?
- Is the data period and scope consistent across all categories or subgroups?
- Are calculations, sorting, grouping, or limits visible enough to review?
- Does the chart avoid unsupported causal claims?
- Does the evidence note identify the tool used and reviewer status?

## Evidence boundary

Conceptual teaching visuals may show simplified chart ideas.
They should remain text-light and avoid exact values, fake percentages, misleading axes, and claims of project evidence.
Final charts require source data, assumptions, scope, and review status.
