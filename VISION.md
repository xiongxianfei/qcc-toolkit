# Vision

## Pitch

QCC Toolkit is a template-backed, Python-powered toolkit for building traceable Quality Control Circle project evidence.

It helps teams learn and apply QCC methods through reusable PowerPoint method templates, preserve method knowledge through Markdown guides, and generate reliable project charts through Python-based validation, calculation, chart generation, interpretation, and metadata.

The project does not try to replace QCC PowerPoint templates. It makes those templates more reliable by connecting them to reproducible evidence assets: source data, method parameters, calculated tables, chart specifications, captions, warnings, and metadata.

## What makes this different

Most quality-improvement tooling starts from generic statistics, generic charting, dashboards, or manual office-document preparation. QCC Toolkit starts from the QCC method story and the way QCC teams actually present their work: reusable method templates, project-story slides, and evidence charts.

A chart is useful only when it supports a QCC stage, answers a specific quality question, and creates evidence that can be reviewed, explained, reused, and inserted into QCC materials.

The tradeoff is focus. QCC Toolkit treats PowerPoint as the teaching and presentation surface, Markdown as the method-knowledge surface, and Python as the evidence-generation surface. It is deep enough for QCC method templates, core quality methods, and chart evidence without becoming a general-purpose business intelligence platform, statistical IDE, enterprise quality system, no-code desktop application, real-time operational dashboard, or document editor.

## Who it is for

QCC Toolkit is for QCC facilitators, quality engineers, improvement teams, educators, and analysts who already teach, learn, review, or present QCC work through method templates and project-story materials.

It is especially for users who want to keep the convenience of reusable QCC PowerPoint templates while improving the reliability, traceability, and reproducibility of the charts and evidence inserted into those templates.

## Who it is not for

QCC Toolkit is not for teams looking for a no-code desktop application, a broad enterprise quality management system, a CAPA or EQMS replacement, a real-time operational dashboard, a full replacement for specialized statistical software, or isolated chart helpers with no QCC workflow context.

It is also not for teams that want manually edited slide charts to be the only record for data-dependent QCC conclusions.

## What it commits to

QCC Toolkit commits to keeping QCC stage workflow visible in templates, method guides, generated charts, and report-ready evidence.

It commits to treating PowerPoint method templates as first-class user-facing assets for teaching layout, presentation structure, demo examples, and copyable QCC slide patterns.

It commits to treating Markdown method guides as the canonical explanation surface for method purpose, usage guidance, review checklists, method-selection advice, and common mistakes.

It commits to treating Python method contracts as the authority for data validation, formulas, calculations, chart specifications, generated captions, warnings, and metadata.

It commits to treating charts as evidence objects. A chart carries its project stage, method, source data context, filters, assumptions, calculation method, interpretation, and report purpose.

It commits to reproducible outputs. A final data-dependent chart can be traced back to source data, method ID, parameters, filters, calculation version, chart specification, output file, and toolkit version.

It commits to a canonical QCC Story stage model with configurable display terminology, so local labels can change without fragmenting method, template, chart, and evidence records.

## What it refuses to be

QCC Toolkit refuses to become a loose collection of unrelated plotting, statistics, template, or slide assets with QCC branding added afterward.

It refuses to treat manually edited slide charts as the authoritative record for data-dependent QCC conclusions.

It refuses to generate charts without preserving the assumptions, data context, method logic, and project-stage relevance needed to review them.

It refuses to let PowerPoint templates, notebooks, or starter scripts duplicate formulas or override the method engine for final evidence.

It refuses to prioritize dashboard breadth, document editing, generic business charts, broad CAPA workflows, or early ERP and EQMS integrations over QCC method clarity and evidence quality.

## What would prove this wrong

The vision is wrong if users mainly adopt the project as a generic charting or statistics utility and ignore its QCC method-template workflow.

It is wrong if PowerPoint templates, Markdown guides, Python scripts, and generated evidence drift into separate and contradictory sources of truth.

It is wrong if final QCC slides still contain data-dependent charts that cannot explain their source data, assumptions, calculation method, interpretation, and project-stage relevance.

It is wrong if common QCC methods remain easier to apply, inspect, reproduce, and explain through disconnected spreadsheets, manually edited slides, and ad hoc scripts.
